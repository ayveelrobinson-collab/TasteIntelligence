PERCEPTION
Trend Command Center · Real-Time Taste Signals
Primary buyer: Strategy / Insights
Dashboard Purpose
"What is happening in food right now — and where is it happening?"
Perception is the real-time intelligence layer. It tells you which ingredients are rising or falling, which flavor combinations are gaining momentum, where in the country taste is shifting, and which consumer archetypes are driving the change. It's the platform's always-on radar — the first screen you open every morning.
Live Signals Ticker Always-on
A scrolling real-time feed at the top of the screen showing all active ingredient signals — their current momentum score and month-over-month change. Modeled after a financial ticker.
How to read it: High score + positive MoM = rising fast. Low score + negative MoM = declining. Use to quickly spot what to dig into first.
KPI Cards (top row)
Five headline numbers: total signals detected, count of rising ingredients, count of declining trends, emerging flavor pairs, and active geo markets. Counts animate on load.
How to read it: Rising ingredients increasing faster than declining = healthy, expanding signal environment. Geo markets active shows national vs regional concentration.
Ingredient Trend Signals Core panel
The main table. Every tracked ingredient ranked by Momentum Score (1–100), with a 10-point inline sparkline showing its 30-day trajectory, and MoM % change. Filterable by Rising / All / Declining.
How to read it: Score above 80 = high-momentum signal. The sparkline shape matters as much as the number — a flat 85 is less interesting than a sharply rising 70. MoM % shows velocity.
Flavor Pair Momentum
Tracks which ingredient combinations are emerging together on menus and in consumer data. Each pair shows momentum label (Very High / High / Rising / Emerging / Watch), a percentage bar, and a velocity index (how much faster than baseline it's growing).
How to read it: Velocity index above 1.5× means it's growing meaningfully faster than the average. "Watch" pairs are pre-signal — useful for long-range product planning.
Geo Taste Heatmap
A US bubble map where circle size = signal intensity and color = signal strength tier (High/Med/Low). Hover any city for a tooltip showing top trending ingredients and 30-day trend in that market.
How to read it: Large amber circles = high signal markets where trends are arriving or accelerating. Use this to prioritize pilot launch cities and regional marketing spend.
Occasion Intelligence
Signal strength indexed to consumption occasion — Morning Ritual, Weekend Dining, Bar & Cocktails, etc. Score shows how much taste signal is concentrated in each moment.
How to read it: High score on Weekend Dining = opportunity for premium, experiential menu items. High score on Casual Weeknight = opportunity for accessible, repeatable formats.
Taste Archetypes (left panel)
The four consumer clusters segmented by flavor preference behavior: Explorer, Comfort Seeker, Conscious Eater, Prestige Diner. Each has a dominance percentage and associated flavor tags. Selecting an archetype filters the signal view to that segment.
How to read it: Explorer is the leading edge — trends show up in Explorer data 3–6 months before they reach Comfort Seekers. Use as an early-warning filter.
Signal Alerts (right panel)
Automatically generated notifications when a signal crosses a threshold. Five alert types: Surge (rapid rise), Spike (sudden spike), Crossover (trend entering new channel), New (first detection), Decline (falling signal). Each includes market and segment badges.
How to read it: Crossover alerts are the highest-value signal — they indicate a trend moving from one channel (e.g. fine dining) into another (e.g. QSR). Act on these fastest.

VALIDATION
Concept Scoring Report · Concept & Menu Testing
Primary buyer: Product / Ops
Dashboard Purpose
"Will this concept work — and with whom, and where?"
Validation is the decision-support layer. Before spending money on a pilot, prototype, or launch, you run the concept through Validation. It scores the concept on two independent dimensions, breaks down appeal by demographic and geography, compares it against category benchmarks, and surfaces risk flags. The output is a clear, defensible recommendation: proceed, modify, or kill.
Score Interpretation Scale
0–59
Weak
Do not proceed. Significant reformulation needed.
60–69
Below Average
Niche appeal only. Proceed with caution.
70–79
Average
Viable. Consider modifications before pilot.
80–89
Strong
Recommend pilot. Monitor closely.
90–100
Exceptional
High confidence. Fast-track to launch.
Taste Fit Score Core score
How well the concept's flavor profile, ingredients, and format align with the target consumer segment's actual taste preferences, as measured from Meal Ticket taste profiles and Atlas data.
How to read it: This is about desire — does this concept match what the target consumer actually wants to eat? High Taste Fit but low Demand Fit = great product, wrong timing.
Demand Fit Score Core score
Whether sufficient market demand exists for the concept in the current environment — accounting for trend trajectory, competitive saturation, and market timing.
How to read it: This is about timing — is the market ready? High Demand Fit but low Taste Fit = strong market pull, wrong execution. Both high = launch-ready.
Composite Score
A weighted combination of Taste Fit and Demand Fit, calibrated against the Atlas benchmark dataset of 847K menu items. The single number a decision-maker can act on.
How to read it: Composite of 80+ = recommend proceed to pilot. The written verdict below the score translates this into a plain-English action recommendation.
Demographic Breakdown
Taste Fit score segmented by age group (Gen Z, Millennials, Gen X, Boomers) and market type (Urban, Suburban). Shows where the concept has strong vs weak resonance.
How to read it: A 92 with Gen Z and 51 with Boomers tells you this is a leading-edge concept, not a mass-market one. Use to target your pilot location and marketing.
Variant Comparison (A/B)
Up to 5 concept variants scored against each other using the same model. Shows which modification — ingredient swap, format change, preparation technique — produces the highest composite score.
How to read it: The highest-scoring variant is flagged BEST. Use this to decide which version to take to a physical pilot, saving R&D cycles.
Geographic Appetite
Predicted demand fit score by city market. Tells you where the concept will land best — and where to avoid wasting launch budget.
How to read it: Score above 85 = strong pilot market. Score below 70 = low demand signal — wait for market to mature or don't enter. Start pilots in green markets.
Category Benchmark
How the concept compares against similar items in the Atlas database — including competitor concepts, the category average, and the top-10% benchmark.
How to read it: Scoring above category average is table stakes. Scoring near the Top 10% benchmark means you have a differentiated concept worth prioritizing resources on.
Risk Flags
Automatically surfaced risks: ingredient supply chain constraints, trend saturation windows, allergen conflicts, and competitive activity detected in Atlas. Three risk levels: High, Medium, Low.
How to read it: High risk flags don't mean don't launch — they mean you need a mitigation plan. A 12-month saturation window tells you to move fast, not to stop.

FORESIGHT
Executive Trend Briefing · Predictive Intelligence
Primary buyer: Exec / Strategy
Dashboard Purpose
"What will happen in food over the next 6–24 months — and what should we do about it?"
Foresight is the strategic intelligence layer. It's designed for C-suite and strategy team briefings — high signal, low noise, action-ready. It surfaces where macro trends are in their lifecycle, what whitespace opportunities exist before competitors see them, and provides a 12-month forward forecast with confidence scores. The key differentiator: Taste Intelligence surfaces these signals 6.2 months ahead of trade media on average.
Lead Time Advantage
The average number of months by which Taste Intelligence detects and validates a trend before it appears in mainstream trade publications, social aggregators, or category reports.
How to read it: Currently 6.2 months. This is the core commercial proposition — the window in which clients can act before competitors. Every month this grows, the platform becomes more valuable.
Forecast Accuracy
The rolling 12-month accuracy rate of Foresight's trend trajectory predictions, measured against what actually happened in the market. Currently 89% based on closed-loop validation.
How to read it: This is the platform's credibility number. Use it in sales contexts. Higher accuracy = more client trust in forward forecasts = larger contract value.
Macro Trend Cards
The 5 highest-momentum, highest-longevity trends currently tracked by Foresight, ranked by a combined momentum × longevity score. Each card shows: phase label, momentum %, current penetration %, opportunity window, and confidence score. Includes a miniature lifecycle curve.
How to read it: Phase labels indicate maturity — Emerging Signal = years of runway; Mainstream Entry = 12–18 months before saturation. Act fastest on "Acceleration" phase trends.
Trend Phase Labels
Five lifecycle stages: Emerging Signal (chef/event data only), Early Adoption (indie restaurants), Acceleration (chain adoption), Mainstream Entry (QSR/retail crossover), Saturation (mass market, declining margins).
How to read it: The ideal entry point for a new product is Acceleration phase. Too early (Emerging) = market not ready. Too late (Mainstream Entry) = you're playing catch-up.
12-Month Forward Forecast
A multi-line chart projecting the indexed momentum score of the top 5 ingredients over the next 12 months. The shaded area (when visible) represents the confidence interval. A "Now" line divides historical from projected data.
How to read it: Lines crossing = two trends converging or diverging in significance. A steep upward trajectory that hasn't peaked = significant opportunity. Flatten or decline = past peak.
Whitespace Opportunities
Identified gaps where consumer taste demand is building but no scaled product or concept yet exists to meet it. Each opportunity shows: estimated market size, entry window (time before competition enters), supporting signals, and a confidence score.
How to read it: Short window (12 months) + high confidence = immediate action required. Long window (24+ months) + lower confidence = put on watchlist, monitor quarterly.
12-Month Horizon (sidebar)
A chronological list of predicted market events — trend breaks, saturation points, crossover moments — ranked by timing and confidence score. Arrows indicate direction: ↑ accelerating, ↓ declining, → stabilizing.
How to read it: This is the editorial calendar for product and campaign planning. Map launches and marketing moments against these predicted events.
Watchlist
User-curated list of ingredients and trends being monitored for emergence. Each item has a status tag: ACT (move now), WATCH (3–6 months), or MONITOR (longer horizon, periodic check-in).
How to read it: ACT items should be in active product briefs. WATCH items should be in quarterly planning reviews. MONITOR items feed the long-range innovation pipeline.

CREATION
Product Simulation · R&D Concept Generation
Primary buyer: R&D / Innovation
Dashboard Purpose
"What should we build next — and what will it do commercially before we spend a dollar on development?"
Creation is the product development simulation layer. R&D and innovation teams use it to generate new product or dish concepts from taste signals, simulate their commercial performance before any physical prototype is made, model adoption curves under different market scenarios, and position new concepts against the existing portfolio. It closes the loop back to Validation — every generated concept can be sent directly to Validation for full scoring.
Generation Parameters (left panel)
The input layer for concept generation. Set: target segment, product category, consumption occasion, price point, lead ingredients (Perception signals), must-avoid ingredients, must-include ingredients, and complexity level.
How to read it: More specific parameters = more focused concepts. Use "Lead with Signals" to anchor generation to trending ingredients detected in Perception. Import directly from Foresight to generate concepts from whitespace opportunities.
Generated Concept Cards
The output of a generation run — up to 5 AI-generated food or product concepts, ranked by predicted composite score. Each card shows: concept name, description, flavor/format tags, and revenue proxy estimate.
How to read it: Cards are ranked best-first. Select any card to load its full simulation data in the panels below. The revenue proxy is a directional estimate, not a guarantee — validate with the Validation module.
Adoption Curve Simulation
A 24-month projected market adoption curve for the selected concept under three scenarios: Bull (best case), Base (most likely), and Bear (worst case). Y-axis is penetration index (0–100). Toggle between scenarios to see the range of outcomes.
How to read it: The shape of the curve matters as much as the endpoint. A steep early ramp (J-curve) = viral potential. A slow linear rise = steady but predictable. Compare Bull vs Bear gap to assess risk range.
Revenue Proxy Model
First-year revenue estimates under four scenarios: Optimistic, Base Case, Conservative, and 3-Year TAM. Based on comparable Atlas menu items, segment size, predicted penetration, and average check or price point.
How to read it: Use the Base Case for budget planning and the range (Conservative → Optimistic) for risk assessment. The 3-Year TAM shows the full category ceiling if the concept achieves national scale.
Segment Fit Scores
Predicted appeal score for the selected concept across 7 consumer segments: the four taste archetypes plus Urban, Gen Z, and Family. Shows where the concept has natural pull vs where it will need marketing support.
How to read it: Identify your primary segment (highest score) and secondary segment (second highest). Your launch strategy, marketing voice, and distribution channel should be built around those two segments.
Portfolio Positioning Map
A 2×2 bubble chart mapping new concepts (amber) against existing portfolio items (grey) on two axes: Price/Premium (Y) and Novelty/Familiarity (X). Bubble size represents revenue proxy. Reveals whitespace gaps in the current lineup.
How to read it: Empty quadrants in your portfolio map = opportunity. New concepts that cluster on top of existing items = cannibalization risk. Look for amber bubbles in unoccupied spaces.
AI Insights (right panel)
Three automatically generated insights for the active concept: one Opportunity (whitespace or timing advantage), one Risk Flag (saturation, competition, supply), and one Timing Signal (urgency of the entry window).
How to read it: Read these before any decision. The Risk Flag is particularly important — it's the thing most likely to make or break the concept's commercial performance.
Iteration Engine
A list of AI-suggested modifications to the active concept — ingredient swaps, technique changes, component additions — each with a predicted score delta if applied. Hit "Apply" to incorporate the change and recalculate.
How to read it: Focus on changes with the highest positive delta. Running 2–3 iterations before sending to Validation typically adds 4–8 composite score points — the equivalent of skipping an entire R&D cycle.
Platform Glossary

Terms used across all four modules. Reference these when explaining the platform to prospects, clients, or new team members.

Atlas
The platform's canonical knowledge graph — connecting 847K+ menu items, 1M+ SKUs, ingredients, flavor compounds, and restaurant archetypes. Every module reads from Atlas; Crave and Capture write back into it.
Capture
The event and operator data acquisition layer. Collects proprietary taste signals from 100–300 food festivals and chef showcases annually, plus POS integrations and chef plate profile submissions. Feeds Atlas.
Meal Ticket
The consumer data layer. A B2C app where users build a Foodie Profile via taste quiz, receive personalized restaurant and dish recommendations, and generate continuous behavioral signals that feed Atlas and Pulse models.
Crave
The commercial Validation product AND an operator data pipeline. Every menu submission, concept score, and A/B test result generated through Crave enriches the Atlas knowledge graph with real-world operator behavior.
Momentum Score
A 0–100 index measuring the current velocity of an ingredient or flavor trend. Combines menu penetration rate, year-over-year growth, Meal Ticket consumer demand signals, and event detection frequency.
Taste Fit Score
How well a food concept aligns with the target consumer segment's measured taste preferences. Derived from Meal Ticket taste profiles matched against the concept's ingredient and flavor profile in Atlas.
Demand Fit Score
Whether sufficient market demand exists for a concept right now. Accounts for trend phase, competitive saturation, regional appetite, and timing relative to the adoption curve.
Composite Score
A weighted combination of Taste Fit and Demand Fit scores, normalized against the full Atlas benchmark dataset. The single decision-support number for go/no-go concept decisions.
Recipe Genome
A normalized archetype representing a dish category in Atlas. Each Genome encodes the flavor profile, technique, cuisine origin, and ingredient relationships — enabling cross-concept comparison and matching.
Flavor Pair Momentum
The velocity at which two ingredients are appearing together on menus and in consumer data. Expressed as a velocity index (e.g. +1.9×) showing how much faster the pairing is growing vs baseline.
Whitespace Opportunity
A gap where consumer taste demand is measurably building but no scaled product or concept yet exists to meet it. Foresight surfaces these with estimated market size and entry window before competition arrives.
Trend Phase
Five lifecycle stages: Emerging Signal → Early Adoption → Acceleration → Mainstream Entry → Saturation. Determines both the urgency of action and the type of product strategy appropriate at each stage.
Lead Time Advantage
The average number of months by which Taste Intelligence detects and validates a trend before it appears in mainstream trade publications. Currently 6.2 months — the core commercial value of the platform.
Revenue Proxy
A directional revenue estimate generated by the Creation module for a new concept. Based on comparable Atlas items, target segment size, predicted adoption curve, and average price point. Not a financial projection — a planning signal.
Taste Archetype
One of four consumer behavioral clusters: Explorer (novelty-seeking, global flavors), Comfort Seeker (familiar, indulgent), Conscious Eater (functional, plant-based), Prestige Diner (premium, origin-led). Explorer is the leading-edge signal group.
Iteration Engine
A Creation module feature that suggests specific modifications to a concept (ingredient swaps, technique changes) with predicted score deltas. Enables rapid digital iteration before physical R&D begins.
